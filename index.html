01What it is

A new EU directive that's mandatory on June 19, 2026.

Directive (EU) 2023/2673, the Right of Withdrawal, requires every ecommerce business selling into the EU to offer a clear, easily-found digital withdrawal option. It's separate from your normal returns flow and the rules are stricter.

02Who it affects

Any brand that ships to an EU address.

It applies based on the customer's location, not where your company is based. US, UK, Australia, anywhere. If you ship into the EU, you're in scope. Even one EU shipment triggers it.

03What it requires

Six specific things, all mandatory.

  • A dedicated digital withdrawal flow, separate from regular returns
  • 14-day withdrawal window from the delivery date
  • Two-step confirmation with an automatic email
  • Refund within 14 days to the original payment method
  • Refund shipping, duties, and taxes, not just the product
  • No forced account creation for guest checkouts

04The penalty

Up to 4% of your annual turnover.

Enforcement is by each EU member state's consumer protection authority. Penalties vary by country, but the ceiling stays the same everywhere.

4%
That's the cap on every fine. No warning period. No grace window after June 19, 2026.
