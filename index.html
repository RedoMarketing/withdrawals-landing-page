FAQ

Common questions.

What is the EU Right of Withdrawal?

Directive (EU) 2023/2673 requires every ecommerce business selling to EU consumers to offer a clear, easily-found digital withdrawal option. Customers can withdraw any purchase within 14 days of delivery and get a full refund — including shipping, duties, and taxes.

When does the law take effect?

June 19, 2026. After that date, any EU order processed without a compliant withdrawal option is a liability. There's no grace period.

Who has to comply?

Any ecommerce merchant — anywhere in the world — that ships to an EU address. The rule applies based on the customer's location, not your company's. US, UK, Australia, anywhere — if you ship into the EU, you're in scope.

What's the penalty for non-compliance?

Up to 4% of your annual turnover per the directive. Enforcement falls to each EU member state's consumer protection authority, so penalties vary by country — but the ceiling is the same everywhere.

How is this different from regular returns?

It's a stricter, separate flow with shorter deadlines, mandatory refund of shipping and duties, no return-condition requirements, and no restocking fees. Most existing returns portals don't meet all of these rules — which is why the law specifically requires a dedicated withdrawal option.

Do I really have to refund shipping too?

Yes. The full original outbound shipping cost must be refunded if the customer exercises their withdrawal right. Duties and taxes the customer paid are also refundable on cross-border orders.

What if I only ship a small number of orders to the EU?

Volume doesn't matter. Even one EU shipment triggers the requirement. Redo's flow auto-detects EU orders so you only pay attention to it when it actually applies.
